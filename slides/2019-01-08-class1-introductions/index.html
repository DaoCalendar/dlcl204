Important disclaimer:

Digital humanities is not only about texts

But we're only going to be working with text, and images of text.

Why?

We're (probably) all working in text-centric disciplines. 

It's computationally easier to analyze text.

Text presents plenty of interesting challenges, many related to language.

You don't have to identify as a "digital humanist"

Not now, not at the end of the course.

And how you frame your work relative to digital humanities can vary depending on the job opportunity, funding source, department, local politics, etc. 

(DH is subject to the dynamics of gender, race, prestige, labor, job status, etc. as the rest of the academy. Differences in cultural norms across countries adds further complications.)

Technical

Become familiar with...

  • Text digitization
  • Text visualization tools
  • Reading tech. documentation & filing bug reports
  • Unicode
  • Ways of building thematic research collections
  • Text Encoding Initiative & digital critical editions
  • Topic modeling

Goals for this course

  • Accessing corpora
  • Natural language processing tools for your language
  • Data cleaning
  • Geospatial tools and approaches
  • Network analysis
  • Accessibility in the context of scholarly projects
  • Poster design

Social

Become familiar with...

  • the literary research questions that DH methods can help you answer
  • major national, international, and linguistically-focused DH organizations
  • cross-cultural challenges when collaborating internationally

Goals for this course

  • DH as a tool for activism
  • the difference between text and "data"
  • how personal and professional identities shape DH
  • the nature and role of "infrastructure" for digital humanities
  • the uptake and culture of applying DH methods in your discipline
