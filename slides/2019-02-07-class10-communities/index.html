Why an ACH conference?

Priorities

Your discipline

  • Everyone (more or less) speaks the same language
  • Most people (more or less) share the same methodologies
  • Familiar topics of study (or exciting new things in a familiar framework)
  • People care about the content as such
  • More can be left implicit

DH conference

  • Many languages
  • Many methodologies and scholarly traditions
  • Many diverse topics
  • Interest in what can vary; common ground is how
  • Emphasis on "novelty" (but you have to take people's word for it)
  • Have to be explicit about why it matters
  • Librarians/IT/alt-ac people engage wearing those hats

Tips for writing a DH proposal

  • Assume nothing, gloss everything
  • Contextualize everything from your discipline, ideally also from DH
  • Be clear about what you're trying to argue for/against, and/or why what you're describing is significant
  • Say what tools you're using
    • ​If there's major, widely-known shortcomings, be clear why they're not a problem or how you're mitigating them

Tips for writing a DH proposal

  • Say what your corpus is
  • What's "novel" about what you're doing?
    • Don't overstate this. Grandiosity is bait for reviewers to poke holes in it.
  • Relativize your conclusions to your corpus, but gesture at larger significance if you can (especially within a DH context)
